Glow claims that the traditional security tools we’ve been using for years are outdated. They are building a new platform designed specifically for the “AI Era.” As companies rush to integrate Artificial Intelligence into their workflows, they are opening up massive new security holes that old-school antivirus software just can’t plug.

**[BACKGROUND CONTEXT]**
To understand why Glow is worth over a billion dollars, we have to look at the current state of cybersecurity. 

Right now, the industry is dominated by massive players like CrowdStrike and SentinelOne. They’ve done a great job, but their systems are built for a world where people logged into office computers and stayed within a secure network. 

Today, everyone is working remotely, using cloud-based apps, and running AI models that scrape and process massive amounts of sensitive data. Hackers have evolved. They’re no longer just sending phishing emails; they’re using AI to find tiny cracks in enterprise systems. Glow is positioning itself as the “next generation” of defense—an AI-native security firm designed to protect companies from the very technologies that are supposed to be helping them.

Why should you care about a billion-dollar security startup? Because your data is the target.
